A comparison of various charting frameworks. Currently they're just asked to render 100,000 data points as a line chart.

  • D3 - Popular, successful OSS, well designed, svg-based.
  • Highcharts - Popular, feature-rich, not free, svg-based.
  • Dygraphs - Canvas-based, over 5 years old, code is soupy but well tested.
  • Rickshaw - Based on D3, just a sugary layer on top?
  • Custom Canvas - Just testing out the most raw format
  • Chart.js - New, canvas-based, horrible architecture (example: every datapoint needs a visible label). It dies with 100k data points, so it's currently drawing 1k.
